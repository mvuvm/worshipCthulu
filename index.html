Worship Cthulu

Cthulhu (or Tulu, Cthulu) is a fictional creature that occupies one of the most sacred temples of Cthulu mythology. The names are pronounced in various ways, but in Korean, they are usually called "Ktulu." According to Howard Phillips Lovecraft, the name is in an ancient alien language that humans cannot pronounce, and Lovecraft recorded that it would be close to the pronunciation seen by KHUL'HLOO. Cthulu is the ruler of the city of R'lyeh, which sank in the South Pacific, and is an evil deity who will bring disaster to the world with awakening.

Don't make eye contact with him

Physically, Ktulu has a bloated human form and green skin. The head looks like an octopus, and the chin is surrounded by tentacles. It has two bat wings on its back, and its skin is limp and mucous. The size of the ctulu is unknown, with some saying it can increase and decrease in height as much as possible.

According to Lovecraft:

"If I say that my imagination, which is a little absurd, presents a picture in which an octopus, a dragon, and a caricatured human being exist at the same time, I would not be able to say that I was faithful to its essence. A sticky, tentacle-covered head rose over a grotesque, scaly-wrapped torso, with wings left only traces. But it was nothing more than an approximate outline of the astonishingly terrifying whole. Beyond the figure, megalithic structures were dimly visible..." - H.P. Lovecraft, "Cruel's Call"
